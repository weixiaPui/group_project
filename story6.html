Fran H.

Living away from home was a daunting prospect to start with, but after a couple years, it has become more natural. I still remember moving into halls in first year, taking multiple trips from the car to my new room and the numerous stairs that things had to be moved up and down. Meeting new flatmates was an exciting experience and it was great to see how quickly we all bonded. Three years later, it’s great to see that we are all still such great friends and hopefully, this will continue even after university. Second year was an exciting year and looking back maybe the best year. You feel settled at university, you know where you’re going, you have your routine. The work load isn’t too intense, you find the time you go out and enjoy making new friends. It’s also the year, I found that you start enjoying your studies even more having picked optional units. You get to study modules that you find interesting, which also means that numerous readings don’t seem too much of a chore. Seminars begin to be more exciting, not just the sit in there in silence for 50 minutes.
